Cyrillic alphabet

Cyrllic was introduced in the 1940s in the Mongolian People's Republic under Soviet influence. The Cyrillic script had many advantages over the traditional Mongolian including having clear certain letters such as "t" and "d," "o" and "u" which were frequently confused in traditional script, and simple & easy shapes thats consistent regardless of position unlike the Mongolian script.

Why Learn the Traditional Mongolian Script?

Although Cyrillic has been the dominant script since the 1940s, the traditional Mongolian script remains a vital part of Mongolia’s cultural identity. Its elegant vertical form connects modern Mongolians to their rich history, classical literature, and centuries-old traditions. With Mongolia's 2025 bilingual policy requiring both scripts in official documents, learning the traditional script is not just about preserving heritage—it's about preparing for the future.
